Earth Systems: Geographic Evidence

Cross-curricular connection: Students use geographic tools to collect evidence about Earth system interactions and present findings using scientific reasoning.

Subject: Science
Grade: 6-8
Duration: 50 minutes
Integration: Social Studies (Geographic Tools)

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Students will identify the four major Earth systems (geosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, biosphere)
  • Students will use geographic tools to collect evidence of system interactions
  • Students will apply scientific reasoning to explain system relationships
  • Students will connect local Alaska examples to global Earth systems

üìã Standards Alignment

NGSS:

  • 5-ESS2-1: Develop a model using an example to describe ways the geosphere, biosphere, hydrosphere, and/or atmosphere interact
  • MS-ESS2-1: Develop a model to describe the cycling of Earth's materials and the flow of energy that drives this process

Alaska Science Standards:

  • SC5.3.1: The student demonstrates an understanding of cycles influenced by energy from the sun and by Earth's position and motion in our solar system

üìö Materials Needed

  • Satellite images of Alaska (different seasons)
  • Topographic maps of Alaska
  • Weather data charts
  • Earth systems interaction cards
  • Digital mapping tools (Google Earth or similar)
  • Scientific observation sheets

No special safety requirements for this lesson. Standard classroom safety protocols apply.

5 minutes

üöÄ Opening: Phenomenon Introduction

Hook: Show before/after satellite images of the Mendenhall Glacier in Alaska (1999 vs 2019).

Question: "What Earth systems do you think are involved in this change? How could we use geographic tools to study this phenomenon?"

Purpose: Students will investigate Earth system interactions using the same tools geographers and scientists use.

15 minutes

üìñ Direct Instruction: Earth Systems & Geographic Tools

Teach the 4 Earth Systems:

  • Geosphere: Rocky parts of Earth (mountains, soil, rocks)
  • Hydrosphere: All water on Earth (oceans, rivers, ice, groundwater)
  • Atmosphere: Layer of gases surrounding Earth
  • Biosphere: All living things and their environments

Geographic Tools for Scientists:

  • Satellite imagery shows changes over time
  • Topographic maps reveal elevation and landforms
  • Weather data tracks atmospheric conditions
  • GIS technology combines multiple data layers
25 minutes

üîç Investigation: Alaska Earth Systems Evidence

Student Teams (3-4 students): Each team gets a different Alaska location to investigate:

  • Denali National Park
  • Prince William Sound
  • Arctic National Wildlife Refuge
  • Aleutian Islands

Investigation Process:

  1. Observe (8 min): Use satellite images, topographic maps, and weather data to identify all 4 Earth systems in your location
  2. Analyze (10 min): Find evidence of how the systems interact (e.g., how does the geosphere affect the hydrosphere?)
  3. Document (7 min): Record observations using scientific vocabulary and geographic evidence

Guiding Questions:

  • What geographic tools provide the best evidence for each system?
  • How do changes in one system affect the others?
  • What patterns do you notice in the data?
5 minutes

üéØ Closing: Evidence-Based Conclusions

Quick Share: Each team shares one surprising discovery about Earth system interactions in Alaska.

Reflection: "How is studying Earth systems similar to the way we analyze geographic evidence in social studies?"

Preview: Tomorrow in ELA, we'll use this same type of evidence to support our arguments about land use decisions.

üìä Assessment

Formative:

  • Circulate during investigation to check understanding
  • Review team observation sheets
  • Listen to team discussions about system interactions

Summative: Students complete exit ticket explaining how two Earth systems interact in their assigned Alaska location, supported by geographic evidence.

üîÑ Differentiation

Support: Provide graphic organizers for system identification and interaction tracking

Extension: Advanced students research how climate change affects Earth system interactions in Alaska

ELL Support: Include visual vocabulary cards for Earth systems and geographic tools

Technology Alternative: If digital tools unavailable, use printed maps and images with magnifying glasses

üè† Homework/Extension

Students observe their local environment (school grounds, neighborhood) and identify examples of all four Earth systems. They should note any interactions they observe and bring findings to share tomorrow.
