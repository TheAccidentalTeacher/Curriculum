üî¨ Learning Activities

10 minutes

Opening: Connect to ELA Research

  • Review research questions about Alaska population from ELA class
  • Ask: "How do scientists investigate questions about Earth and geography?"
  • Introduce: Geographic investigations use the scientific method
Scientific Method Steps:
  1. Observation
  2. Question
  3. Hypothesis
  4. Investigation/Experiment
  5. Data Collection
  6. Analysis
  7. Conclusion
15 minutes

Mini-Lesson: Scientific Method in Geography

  • Example investigation: "Why do some areas of Alaska have more rainfall than others?"
    • Observation: Different rainfall amounts across Alaska
    • Question: What causes these differences?
    • Hypothesis: Mountains and ocean proximity affect rainfall
    • Investigation: Collect rainfall data and geographic features
  • Discuss how geographers use data, maps, and tools as evidence
  • Introduce vocabulary: hypothesis, variable, data, evidence
20 minutes

Hands-On Investigation Planning

Investigation Question: "How does elevation affect temperature in Alaska?"

  • Students work in groups to plan their investigation:
    • Write a hypothesis about elevation and temperature
    • Identify what data they need to collect
    • List tools and resources required
    • Plan how they will analyze the data
  • Groups record their plans in investigation worksheets
  • Teacher circulates and guides scientific thinking
15 minutes

Data Collection Simulation

  • Provide temperature and elevation data for 10 Alaska locations
  • Students create data tables and record information
  • Practice making observations vs. inferences
  • Begin creating simple graphs or charts
10 minutes

Analysis & Conclusion

  • Groups analyze their data and identify patterns
  • Compare results to their original hypothesis
  • Discuss: "What did we learn? What questions do we still have?"
  • Introduce concept of peer review and sharing results
5 minutes

Closing: Real-World Applications

  • Discuss how scientists use this process to study climate change, natural disasters, and resource management
  • Preview tomorrow: How do we measure and scale geographic features?
