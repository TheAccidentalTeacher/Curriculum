üìö Learning Activities

10 minutes

Opening: Review & Connect

  • Review yesterday's geographic tools discussion
  • Ask: "What questions do you have about your local area that could be answered using maps or data?"
  • Brainstorm research questions as a class
15 minutes

Mini-Lesson: Research Process

  • Introduce the research cycle: Question ‚Üí Sources ‚Üí Evidence ‚Üí Analysis ‚Üí Conclusion
  • Discuss credible sources for geographic information:
    • Government agencies (USGS, NOAA, Census Bureau)
    • Academic institutions and research organizations
    • Peer-reviewed articles and databases
    • Primary sources (surveys, interviews, observations)
  • Teach source evaluation criteria: Authority, Accuracy, Currency, Purpose
25 minutes

Guided Research Practice

Research Question: "How has the population of Alaska changed over the past 50 years, and what factors might explain these changes?"

  • Students work in pairs to:
    • Identify 3-4 credible sources
    • Gather population data from different time periods
    • Look for information about economic, environmental, or social factors
    • Record sources using proper citation format
  • Teacher circulates and provides guidance on source selection
  • Students create evidence organizer with findings
15 minutes

Evidence Analysis & Sharing

  • Pairs analyze their evidence and identify patterns
  • Write a 3-sentence summary of their findings
  • Share findings with another pair
  • Discuss: "What additional questions do your findings raise?"
5 minutes

Closing: Reflection

  • Quick write: "What was challenging about finding credible sources? What strategies helped?"
  • Preview tomorrow: Using maps to solve real-world problems

üìä Assessment

Formative Assessment:

  • Source evaluation using AACP criteria (Authority, Accuracy, Currency, Purpose)
  • Quality of evidence gathered and organized
  • Participation in pair work and class discussions

Summative Assessment:

  • Evidence organizer completeness and accuracy (40%)
  • Proper citation format (20%)
  • Quality of analysis and summary (30%)
  • Use of geographic vocabulary (10%)

üè† Homework & Extension

  • Required: Find one additional credible source about Alaska population changes and add to evidence organizer
  • Extension: Research population changes in your specific community or region
  • Family Connection: Interview family member about changes they've observed in Alaska
  • Reading: Review map reading basics for tomorrow's lesson

üéì Teacher Notes

  • Differentiation: Provide pre-selected sources for struggling students, challenge advanced students to find primary sources
  • Technology: If limited internet, provide printed materials from credible sources
  • Alaska Context: Emphasize local relevance and connect to students' experiences
  • Science Connection: Reinforce scientific method approach to geographic questions
  • Materials: Source evaluation checklist, citation format guide, evidence organizer template
