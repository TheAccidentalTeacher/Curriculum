üìö Learning Activities

15 minutes

Opening: Alaska Climate Connection

  • Review climate data from Anchorage, Fairbanks, and Barrow
  • Quick-write: "How does Alaska's climate compare to what you see in global data?"
  • Share observations about temperature ranges and precipitation patterns
25 minutes

Data Analysis Workshop

  • Students work in pairs to analyze climate data sets from 6 global cities
  • Use graphic organizers to record patterns in:
    • Temperature ranges (monthly averages)
    • Precipitation patterns (seasonal distribution)
    • Extreme weather events (frequency and intensity)
  • Identify trends and make connections between latitude and climate
  • Note vocabulary: latitude, precipitation, temperature range, seasonal variation
30 minutes

Analytical Writing Task

Prompt: "Based on the climate data you analyzed, write a 300-word analytical essay explaining how latitude affects climate patterns. Use specific data from at least three different cities to support your claims."

  • Structure: Introduction with claim, 2-3 body paragraphs with evidence, conclusion
  • Requirements:
    • Include specific temperature and precipitation data
    • Use transition words to connect ideas
    • Define technical terms clearly
    • Connect to Alaska examples where relevant
  • Vocabulary focus: latitude, precipitation, temperature gradient, seasonal variation, climate zone
15 minutes

Peer Review & Revision

  • Students exchange drafts and provide feedback using rubric
  • Focus on: clear claims, specific evidence, scientific vocabulary use
  • Make revisions based on peer feedback
  • Prepare for sharing with science class
5 minutes

Closing Connection

  • Preview tomorrow's lesson on climate modeling predictions
  • Reflect: "How might climate change affect the patterns you identified?"
  • Assign: Complete final draft for science class collaboration

üìä Assessment

Formative Assessment:

  • Data analysis graphic organizers (completeness and accuracy)
  • Peer feedback quality during revision process
  • Participation in climate pattern discussions

Summative Assessment:

  • Analytical essay rubric focusing on:
    • Clear claim about latitude-climate relationship (25%)
    • Specific data evidence from multiple sources (25%)
    • Appropriate use of scientific vocabulary (25%)
    • Writing clarity and organization (25%)

Alaska Context Assessment:

  • Students can explain how Alaska's climate fits into global patterns
  • Use local examples to support broader climate concepts

üè† Homework & Extension

  • Required: Finalize analytical essay draft for science class sharing
  • Extension: Research one climate change impact on Alaska and prepare 2-minute presentation
  • Family Connection: Interview family member about climate changes they've observed in Alaska
  • Reading: Preview next unit materials on human geography

üéì Teacher Notes

  • Differentiation: Provide simplified data sets for struggling readers, advanced students analyze seasonal variations
  • Technology: If available, use online climate databases; otherwise use printed charts
  • Science Collaboration: Share student essays with science teacher for climate modeling lesson
  • Rural Considerations: Use familiar Alaska locations and weather patterns as anchor examples
  • Materials Needed: Climate data packets, graphic organizers, peer review rubrics
