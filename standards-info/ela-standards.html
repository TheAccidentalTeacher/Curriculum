üìö Reading Standards Integration

6th-8th Grade Reading

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RST.6-8.1: Cite specific textual evidence

Integration: Students read geographic texts, research articles, and primary sources to support their geographic analyses and arguments.

Curriculum Example: When studying climate change impacts, students cite specific data from Alaska climate research reports to support their arguments about global climate patterns, learning to evaluate source credibility and relevance.
6th-8th Grade Reading

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RST.6-8.7: Integrate visual information with text

Integration: Students analyze maps, charts, graphs, and satellite imagery alongside text to develop comprehensive understanding.

Curriculum Example: Students read about deforestation while analyzing satellite images showing forest loss over time, then write analytical reports combining visual and textual evidence.

6th-8th Grade Writing

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.1: Write arguments to support claims in an analysis

Integration: Students write argumentative essays about geographic issues, supporting claims with evidence from multiple sources.

Curriculum Example: Students write argumentative essays about sustainable development in Alaska, using evidence from environmental studies, economic data, and cultural perspectives to support their position.
6th-8th Grade Writing

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.2: Write informative/explanatory texts

Integration: Students write research reports, geographic analyses, and explanatory texts about world regions and processes.

Curriculum Example: Students research and write informational reports about water scarcity in different world regions, explaining causes, effects, and potential solutions using geographic evidence.

üìà ELA Skills Progression by Quarter

Our curriculum systematically builds ELA skills through geographic content:

Quarter 1: Foundation Skills

  • Narrative Writing: Personal connections to geographic concepts
  • Reading Comprehension: Geographic texts and primary sources
  • Research Skills: Finding and evaluating geographic information
  • Vocabulary Development: Geographic and academic terminology

Quarter 2: Analysis and Synthesis

  • Expository Writing: Explaining geographic processes and patterns
  • Text Analysis: Comparing multiple sources and perspectives
  • Media Literacy: Analyzing maps, graphs, and multimedia sources
  • Speaking and Listening: Presenting geographic research

Quarter 3: Critical Thinking

  • Argumentative Writing: Taking positions on geographic issues
  • Critical Thinking: Evaluating multiple perspectives
  • Source Evaluation: Assessing credibility and bias
  • Presentation Skills: Formal academic presentations

Quarter 4: Creative Application

  • Creative Writing: Imagining geographic futures
  • Literary Analysis: Geographic themes in literature
  • Project-Based Learning: Extended research projects
  • Reflection and Synthesis: Connecting learning across disciplines

6th-8th Grade Speaking

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.6-8.1: Engage effectively in collaborative discussions

Integration: Students participate in geographic discussions, debates, and collaborative research projects.

Curriculum Example: Students participate in mock UN climate conferences, representing different countries and discussing geographic factors that influence national positions on environmental policies.
6th-8th Grade Speaking

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.6-8.4: Present claims and findings clearly

Integration: Students present geographic research using appropriate academic language and visual aids.

Curriculum Example: Students present research on sustainable cities, using maps, graphs, and multimedia to support their findings about urban planning solutions in different world regions.

üí° Why Geographic Content Enhances ELA Learning

Using geographic content to teach ELA skills provides multiple benefits:

  • Real-world relevance makes writing and reading more meaningful
  • Multiple text types (maps, data, narratives) develop diverse literacy skills
  • Global perspectives expand students' understanding of audience and purpose
  • Cross-curricular connections deepen learning in both subjects
  • Critical thinking about real issues develops higher-order skills

üìä Assessment and Progress Monitoring

ELA progress is assessed through:

  • Writing Portfolios - Collection of geographic writing across genres
  • Reading Comprehension - Understanding of complex geographic texts
  • Oral Presentations - Academic speaking about geographic topics
  • Research Projects - Independent investigation of geographic issues
  • Collaborative Discussions - Participation in geographic debates and analysis
