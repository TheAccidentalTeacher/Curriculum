üí° Why DOK 3-4 Focus Matters

Our curriculum emphasizes higher-order thinking because:

  • Real-world readiness: Students need strategic thinking for college and careers
  • Deeper learning: Analysis and synthesis create lasting understanding
  • Critical thinking: Students learn to evaluate information and make decisions
  • Problem-solving: Complex geographic issues require sophisticated thinking
  • 21st-century skills: Future success requires more than memorization

üìä DOK 3-4 Examples in Our Curriculum

DOK 3 Example: Climate Change Analysis

Task: Students analyze climate data from Alaska and three other world regions to evaluate the statement: "Climate change affects all regions equally."

DOK 3 Verbs Used: analyze, evaluate, compare, justify, critique, defend

Why DOK 3: Requires strategic thinking to compare multiple data sources, draw conclusions, and support arguments with evidence.

DOK 4 Example: Sustainable City Design Project

Task: Students design a sustainable city for a specific climate region, researching environmental constraints, economic factors, and cultural needs over multiple weeks.

DOK 4 Verbs Used: design, synthesize, create, formulate, compose

Why DOK 4: Extended project requiring synthesis of multiple disciplines, sustained research, and creative problem-solving.

DOK 3 Example: Cultural Adaptation Investigation

Task: Students investigate how Alaska Native cultures adapted to Arctic environments and compare these adaptations with indigenous adaptations in other extreme environments worldwide.

DOK 3 Verbs Used: investigate, compare, analyze, conclude, generalize

Why DOK 3: Requires deep analysis of cultural-environmental relationships and sophisticated comparison across cultures.

üéì How We Scaffold to DOK 3-4

We don't skip DOK 1-2, but use them as building blocks:

Scaffolding Example: Water Scarcity Unit

DOK 1-2 Foundation: Students learn vocabulary (scarcity, conservation) and identify water-scarce regions on maps.

DOK 3 Analysis: Students analyze why some regions face water scarcity while others have abundance, comparing climate, geography, and human factors.

DOK 4 Synthesis: Students design comprehensive water management plans for different world regions, considering environmental, economic, and social factors.

üìà Assessment at DOK 3-4 Levels

Higher-order thinking requires different assessment approaches:

  • Performance Tasks: Complex, multi-step problems
  • Research Projects: Extended investigation and synthesis
  • Case Studies: Analysis of real-world geographic issues
  • Design Challenges: Engineering solutions to geographic problems
  • Argumentative Essays: Using evidence to support positions
  • Collaborative Discussions: Defending ideas with evidence

üåü Student Benefits of DOK 3-4 Focus

Students who regularly engage in strategic and extended thinking:

  • Develop critical thinking skills essential for college success
  • Learn to solve complex problems relevant to real-world challenges
  • Build confidence in their ability to tackle difficult questions
  • Prepare for careers that require analysis and innovation
  • Become thoughtful citizens who can evaluate information critically
