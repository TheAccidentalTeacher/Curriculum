üåç Earth Systems Science (ESS)

Middle School Earth Science

MS-ESS2-1: Develop models to describe cycling of Earth's materials

Integration: Students investigate how Earth's materials cycle through different geographic regions, using local Alaska examples and global patterns.

Curriculum Example: Students model the water cycle by comparing precipitation patterns in Alaska's different climate regions with global precipitation patterns, understanding how latitude, altitude, and proximity to water affect local water cycles.
Middle School Earth Science

MS-ESS2-2: Analyze and interpret data on scale properties of objects in the solar system

Integration: Students use geographic tools to understand Earth's place in the solar system and how astronomical factors affect geographic patterns.

Curriculum Example: Students investigate why Alaska experiences extreme daylight variations, using data about Earth's tilt and orbit to explain seasonal patterns and comparing these with patterns in other high-latitude regions worldwide.
Middle School Earth Science

MS-ESS3-3: Apply scientific principles to design solutions for environmental impacts

Integration: Students design solutions to environmental challenges in different world regions, using scientific principles and geographic understanding.

Curriculum Example: Students design sustainable transportation systems for remote Alaska communities, then apply their solutions to similar remote regions worldwide, considering climate, terrain, and economic factors.

üåü Anchoring Phenomena by Quarter

Each quarter uses compelling geographic phenomena to drive scientific investigation:

Quarter 1: Why do places look and feel different?

Phenomena: Students investigate why Alaska has such diverse landscapes and climates within one state, leading to understanding of Earth systems, plate tectonics, and climate patterns.

Quarter 2: How do humans adapt to different environments?

Phenomena: Students explore how people in different climates (Arctic Alaska, tropical Central America, Mediterranean Europe) have developed different technologies and societies, investigating human-environment interactions.

Quarter 3: Why are some places more vulnerable to natural disasters?

Phenomena: Students investigate why Alaska experiences earthquakes while other regions face different hazards, understanding plate tectonics, weather systems, and risk assessment.

Quarter 4: How can we build a sustainable future?

Phenomena: Students design solutions for environmental challenges in Alaska and other regions, applying engineering design process to real-world sustainability problems.

üîß Engineering Design Standards

Middle School Engineering

MS-ETS1-1: Define criteria and constraints of design solutions

Integration: Students design solutions to geographic problems, considering environmental, economic, and social constraints.

Curriculum Example: Students design renewable energy systems for different world regions, considering local climate, resources, and economic conditions as design constraints.
Middle School Engineering

MS-ETS1-2: Evaluate competing design solutions

Integration: Students compare different approaches to geographic challenges, evaluating effectiveness across different contexts.

Curriculum Example: Students evaluate different water conservation strategies used in arid regions worldwide, testing which approaches might work best in different geographic contexts.

üí° Why Geographic Context Enhances Science Learning

Using geographic phenomena to teach science provides multiple benefits:

  • Real-world relevance makes scientific concepts more meaningful
  • Multiple scales help students understand systems thinking
  • Local connections ground abstract concepts in familiar places
  • Global applications show how science applies worldwide
  • Interdisciplinary thinking develops 21st-century skills

üìä Science Assessment Through Geographic Investigation

Scientific understanding is assessed through:

  • Lab Reports - Scientific investigation of geographic phenomena
  • Data Analysis - Interpreting geographic and climate datasets
  • Model Building - Creating representations of Earth systems
  • Engineering Challenges - Designing solutions to geographic problems
  • Scientific Arguments - Using evidence to explain geographic patterns
  • Field Studies - Investigating local geographic phenomena
