üéØ Simulation Focus

Students assume roles as Regional Development Consultants tasked with creating sustainable development strategies for North American regions, balancing economic growth, environmental protection, and cultural preservation.

üìä Comprehensive Assessment Framework

üéØ Standards-Based Assessment Rubric

üåç Geography Standards (Alaska A.1-A.4)

Exemplary (4): Demonstrates comprehensive understanding of geographic concepts, skillfully uses maps and spatial analysis, clearly explains human-environment interactions

Proficient (3): Shows solid understanding of geographic concepts, uses maps effectively, explains most human-environment interactions

Developing (2): Shows basic understanding of geographic concepts, uses maps with assistance, explains some human-environment interactions

Beginning (1): Shows minimal understanding of geographic concepts, needs significant support using maps, limited explanation of interactions

üìñ ELA Standards (CCSS)

Exemplary (4): Writes compelling arguments with strong evidence, presents information clearly and persuasively, integrates technical information skillfully

Proficient (3): Writes clear arguments with adequate evidence, presents information effectively, integrates technical information appropriately

Developing (2): Writes basic arguments with some evidence, presents information clearly, integrates some technical information

Beginning (1): Writes simple arguments with minimal evidence, presents information with support, limited integration of technical information

üß™ Science Standards (NGSS)

Exemplary (4): Applies scientific principles expertly to solve problems, constructs strong evidence-based arguments, evaluates solutions effectively

Proficient (3): Applies scientific principles appropriately, constructs adequate evidence-based arguments, evaluates most solutions

Developing (2): Applies some scientific principles, constructs basic evidence-based arguments, evaluates some solutions

Beginning (1): Applies few scientific principles, constructs minimal arguments, limited evaluation of solutions

üéØ DOK 3-4 Performance

Exemplary (4): Consistently demonstrates strategic and extended thinking, synthesizes complex information, adapts solutions creatively

Proficient (3): Often demonstrates strategic thinking, synthesizes most information, adapts solutions appropriately

Developing (2): Sometimes demonstrates strategic thinking, synthesizes some information, adapts solutions with guidance

Beginning (1): Rarely demonstrates strategic thinking, synthesizes limited information, needs significant support adapting solutions
