Unit Overview

This unit examines Canada's vast geography, multicultural society, and economic development, with emphasis on Canada-Alaska connections and shared Arctic heritage.

6th Grade Lessons üçÅ

  1. Lesson 1: Physical Geography of Canada

    • Objective: Identify Canada's major physical features and regions.
    • Content: Canadian Shield, Rocky Mountains, Arctic, shared border with Alaska.
    • Activities: Canada landform mapping, Alaska-Canada border study.
    • Assessment: Physical features quiz.

  2. Lesson 2: History and Culture

    • Objective: Explore Canada's cultural heritage and development.
    • Content: Indigenous peoples, French and British heritage, multiculturalism.
    • Activities: Cultural mosaic project, First Nations comparison with Alaska Natives.
    • Assessment: Canadian culture presentation.

  3. Lesson 3: Government and Economy

    • Objective: Understand Canada's political and economic systems.
    • Content: Parliamentary system, natural resources, trade with Alaska.
    • Activities: Government comparison chart, resource trade mapping.
    • Assessment: Canada-Alaska connections poster.

  4. Lesson 4: Regional Studies

    • Objective: Compare Canadian provinces and territories.
    • Content: Provinces vs. territories, Yukon-Alaska relationship.
    • Activities: Province/territory fact sheets, Yukon-Alaska sister city research.
    • Assessment: Regional comparison project.

    • Assessment: Bilateral cooperation plan.
