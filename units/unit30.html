üí™ Forms of National Power:

üåü 8th Grade: Advanced Geopolitical Analysis

Lesson 1: Geopolitical Theory and Models

Objective: Students will apply classical geopolitical theories to contemporary international relations.

üìö Geopolitical Theories:

  • Heartland Theory: Control of Eurasia's heartland equals world power
  • Rimland Theory: Coastal areas are more important than interior regions
  • Sea Power Theory: Naval dominance determines global influence
  • Air Power Theory: Aviation and space control in modern warfare
  • Critical Geopolitics: How power shapes geographic knowledge
  • Arctic Geopolitics: Emerging theories for polar regions

üó∫Ô∏è Activity: Geopolitical Theory Application

üìù Quick Check:

Lesson 2: Great Power Competition and Rivalry

Objective: Students will analyze competition between major powers and its effects on global geography.

üåê Great Power Dynamics:

  • Multipolar World: Multiple centers of power competing globally
  • Sphere of Influence: Regions dominated by particular powers
  • Proxy Conflicts: Competition through smaller states and regions
  • Economic Competition: Trade wars, sanctions, and economic influence
  • Technology Competition: Race for technological and digital dominance
  • Arctic Competition: U.S., Russia, and China in the Arctic

üéØ Activity: Arctic Power Competition Analysis

üìù Quick Check:
