Unit Overview

This unit examines human populations, cultures, settlements, economic activities, and political organization around the world, with special attention to Alaska's diverse communities.

6th Grade Lessons üèòÔ∏è

  1. Lesson 1: Population Patterns

    • Objective: Identify where people live and why.
    • Content: Population density, rural vs. urban, Alaska's villages and cities.
    • Activities: Population map creation, local community interview.
    • Assessment: "Where do people live?" poster.

  2. Lesson 2: Cultural Geography

    • Objective: Explore different cultures and traditions.
    • Content: Language, religion, customs, Alaska Native cultures.
    • Activities: Cultural sharing circle, traditional story research.
    • Assessment: Culture comparison chart.

  3. Lesson 3: Settlement Patterns

    • Objective: Understand how settlements develop.
    • Content: Villages, towns, cities, Alaska settlement history.
    • Activities: Settlement timeline, design your ideal community.
    • Assessment: Settlement type identification quiz.

  4. Lesson 4: Economic Activities

    • Objective: Learn about different ways people make a living.
    • Content: Farming, fishing, mining, tourism in Alaska.
    • Activities: Job shadow interview, Alaska economy map.
    • Assessment: "How People Work" presentation.

7th Grade Lessons üåê

  1. Lesson 1: Demographics and Migration

    • Objective: Analyze population trends and movement patterns.
    • Content: Population pyramids, migration patterns, Alaska's changing demographics.
    • Activities: Data graphing, migration story research.
    • Assessment: Population analysis report.

  2. Lesson 2: Cultural Diffusion and Change

    • Objective: Examine how cultures spread and adapt.
    • Content: Cultural diffusion, globalization, preserving traditions.
    • Activities: Cultural exchange simulation, tradition preservation project.
    • Assessment: Cultural change essay.

  3. Lesson 3: Urbanization

    • Objective: Investigate urban growth and challenges.
    • Content: Urban planning, infrastructure, Anchorage case study.
    • Activities: City planning simulation, urban problem-solving.
    • Assessment: Urban development proposal.

  4. Lesson 4: Economic Systems

    • Objective: Compare different economic systems and their impacts.
    • Content: Traditional, command, market economies, Alaska's mixed economy.
    • Activities: Economic system debate, Alaska industry analysis.
    • Assessment: Economic comparison chart.

8th Grade Lessons üèõÔ∏è

  1. Lesson 1: Population Dynamics and Policy

    • Objective: Evaluate population policies and their geographic impacts.
    • Content: Population growth, aging, policy responses, Alaska's rural challenges.
    • Activities: Policy analysis, demographic projection modeling.
    • Assessment: Policy recommendation report.

  2. Lesson 2: Globalization and Identity

    • Objective: Analyze the tension between global and local cultures.
    • Content: Cultural homogenization, resistance, Alaska Native sovereignty.
    • Activities: Globalization impact study, identity preservation strategies.
    • Assessment: Research presentation.

  3. Lesson 3: Sustainable Development

    • Objective: Design sustainable solutions for human settlements.
    • Content: Sustainability principles, green cities, rural Alaska challenges.
    • Activities: Sustainable community design, cost-benefit analysis.
    • Assessment: Sustainability action plan.

  4. Lesson 4: Political Geography

    • Objective: Examine the relationship between politics and geography.
    • Content: Boundaries, sovereignty, governance, Alaska statehood.
    • Activities: Boundary analysis, governance comparison study.
    • Assessment: Political geography portfolio.

Unit Assessment

  • 6th Grade: Culture comparison project, settlement design, community presentation.
  • 7th Grade: Population analysis, urbanization proposal, economic system debate.
  • 8th Grade: Policy recommendation, sustainability plan, political geography portfolio.
