Lesson 1: Types of Transportation

Objective: Students will identify different modes of transportation and their uses.

üöó Transportation Modes:

Land Transportation:
  • Cars and trucks - most common for short distances
  • Trains - good for long distances and heavy cargo
  • Buses - public transportation in cities
  • Bicycles - environmentally friendly for short trips
Water Transportation:
  • Ships - carry large amounts of cargo across oceans
  • Boats and ferries - transport people and goods
  • Barges - move heavy cargo on rivers
Air Transportation:
  • Airplanes - fastest way to travel long distances
  • Helicopters - can land in small spaces
  • Cargo planes - transport goods quickly

üìù Quick Check:

üì¶ Transportation Functions:

üåü 7th Grade: Transportation Networks and Systems

Lesson 1: Transportation Network Theory

Objective: Students will analyze transportation networks and their spatial patterns.

üï∏Ô∏è Network Concepts:

  • Nodes: Points where routes meet (cities, airports, ports)
  • Links: Connections between nodes (roads, flight paths, shipping routes)
  • Connectivity: How well different places are connected
  • Accessibility: Ease of reaching destinations
  • Hub and Spoke: Central hub connects to many smaller points
  • Point-to-Point: Direct connections between destinations

üîó Activity: Alaska Aviation Network Analysis

üìù Quick Check:

üèóÔ∏è Development Factors:
