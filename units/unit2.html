Unit Overview

This unit explores Earth's physical systems, including landforms, water bodies, weather, climate, ecosystems, and natural resources. Students will learn how these systems interact and shape our world.

6th Grade Lessons üèûÔ∏è

  1. Lesson 1: Earth's Landforms

    • Objective: Identify major landforms and describe how they are formed.
    • Content: Mountains, valleys, plains, plateaus, volcanoes.
    • Activities: Landform model building, Alaska landform map.
    • Assessment: Landform identification quiz.

  2. Lesson 2: Water Bodies

    • Objective: Recognize different types of water bodies and their importance.
    • Content: Oceans, rivers, lakes, glaciers, Alaska's waterways.
    • Activities: Water cycle diagram, local waterway research.
    • Assessment: Draw and label a water body.

  3. Lesson 3: Weather and Climate

    • Objective: Explain the difference between weather and climate.
    • Content: Weather patterns, climate zones, Alaska's climate.
    • Activities: Weather journal, climate map coloring.
    • Assessment: Weather vs. climate worksheet.

  4. Lesson 4: Ecosystems and Resources

    • Objective: Explore Alaska's ecosystems and natural resources.
    • Content: Forests, tundra, wildlife, minerals, oil.
    • Activities: Ecosystem diorama, resource scavenger hunt.
    • Assessment: Group presentation: "Our Local Ecosystem."

7th Grade Lessons üå¶Ô∏è

  1. Lesson 1: Plate Tectonics and Landforms

    • Objective: Explain how plate tectonics shape Earth's surface.
    • Content: Plate boundaries, earthquakes, volcanoes, Alaska's geology.
    • Activities: Plate tectonics simulation, Alaska earthquake case study.
    • Assessment: Plate boundary map quiz.

  2. Lesson 2: Hydrosphere and Water Systems

    • Objective: Analyze the role of water in shaping landscapes.
    • Content: Water cycle, rivers, glaciers, permafrost.
    • Activities: Water erosion experiment, Alaska river research.
    • Assessment: Water systems diagram.

  3. Lesson 3: Climate Systems

    • Objective: Describe global and regional climate systems.
    • Content: Climate zones, Alaska's climate, climate change.
    • Activities: Climate graphing, climate change debate.
    • Assessment: Short answer quiz.

  4. Lesson 4: Ecosystems and Human Impact

    • Objective: Investigate how humans affect ecosystems and resources.
    • Content: Resource use, conservation, Alaska case studies.
    • Activities: Resource management simulation, conservation poster.
    • Assessment: Group project: "Protecting Alaska's Resources."

8th Grade Lessons üåé

  1. Lesson 1: Earth Systems Science

    • Objective: Analyze the interactions among Earth's spheres (geosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, biosphere).
    • Content: Earth system models, Alaska as a case study.
    • Activities: System mapping, research project.
    • Assessment: System interaction essay.

  2. Lesson 2: Dynamic Landscapes

    • Objective: Evaluate the processes that shape landscapes over time.
    • Content: Erosion, deposition, glaciation, Alaska's changing land.
    • Activities: Time-lapse analysis, glacial retreat mapping.
    • Assessment: Comparative analysis report.

  3. Lesson 3: Climate Change and Adaptation

    • Objective: Investigate the causes and effects of climate change, with a focus on Alaska.
    • Content: Greenhouse effect, permafrost thaw, adaptation strategies.
    • Activities: Data analysis, adaptation plan design.
    • Assessment: Group presentation: "Adapting to Change."

  4. Lesson 4: Resource Management and Sustainability

    • Objective: Propose sustainable solutions for managing Alaska's resources.
    • Content: Renewable vs. nonrenewable resources, policy, indigenous perspectives.
    • Activities: Policy debate, sustainability action plan.
    • Assessment: Digital portfolio submission.

Unit Assessment

  • 6th Grade: Landform and water body quiz, ecosystem project, reflection.
  • 7th Grade: Plate tectonics map, climate change debate, resource management project.
  • 8th Grade: Earth systems essay, adaptation plan, digital portfolio.
