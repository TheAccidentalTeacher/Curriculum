Unit 24: Natural Resources and Energy

Exploring Earth's Resources and Energy Systems

üåü 6th Grade: Natural Resources Basics

Lesson 1: Types of Natural Resources

Objective: Students will classify natural resources as renewable or nonrenewable.

üèîÔ∏è Natural Resource Categories:

Renewable Resources:
  • Solar energy - energy from the sun
  • Wind energy - power from moving air
  • Water - through the water cycle
  • Forests - trees can be replanted
  • Fish - can reproduce naturally
Nonrenewable Resources:
  • Oil - formed over millions of years
  • Coal - ancient plant matter
  • Natural gas - fossil fuel
  • Metals - copper, gold, iron
  • Minerals - diamonds, salt

‚ôªÔ∏è Activity: Resource Sort

Create a two-column chart listing 20 natural resources. Sort them into renewable and nonrenewable categories. Explain your reasoning for three difficult choices.

üìù Quick Check:

Explain the difference between renewable and nonrenewable resources. Give three examples of each and explain why they fit in that category.

Lesson 2: Alaska's Natural Resources

Objective: Students will identify Alaska's major natural resources and their uses.

üèîÔ∏è Alaska's Resource Wealth:

  • Oil: North Slope, Trans-Alaska Pipeline
  • Natural Gas: Cook Inlet, North Slope
  • Minerals: Gold, zinc, lead, coal
  • Forests: Tongass and Chugach National Forests
  • Fish: Salmon, halibut, crab
  • Tourism: Natural beauty as a resource

üó∫Ô∏è Activity: Alaska Resource Map

Create a map of Alaska showing the locations of major natural resources. Use symbols for oil, gas, minerals, forests, and fishing areas. Include the Trans-Alaska Pipeline.

üìù Quick Check:

List five of Alaska's major natural resources and explain how each one benefits Alaska's economy and people.

Lesson 3: Energy Sources

Objective: Students will compare different energy sources and their environmental impacts.

‚ö° Energy Sources:

  • Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil, natural gas - burned for energy
  • Solar Power: Energy from sunlight using solar panels
  • Wind Power: Wind turbines generate electricity
  • Hydroelectric: Water flowing through dams creates power
  • Geothermal: Heat from inside the Earth

üîã Activity: Energy Source Comparison

Create a chart comparing five energy sources. Include pros and cons for each, plus whether they work well in Alaska's climate and geography.

üìù Quick Check:

Choose two energy sources that would work well in Alaska and explain why they are good choices for Alaska's environment and needs.

Lesson 4: Conservation and Sustainability

Objective: Students will identify ways to conserve natural resources and energy.

üå± Conservation Strategies:

  • Reduce: Use less of a resource
  • Reuse: Find new ways to use items
  • Recycle: Process materials to make new products
  • Energy Conservation: Turn off lights, unplug devices
  • Water Conservation: Fix leaks, take shorter showers

üè† Activity: Home Energy Audit

Conduct an energy audit of your home. List 10 ways your family could save energy and estimate how much energy each action would save.

üìù Assessment: Conservation Action Plan

Create a personal conservation plan with specific actions you will take to save natural resources and energy. Track your progress for one week.

üåü 7th Grade: Resource Distribution and Management

Lesson 1: Global Resource Distribution

Objective: Students will analyze patterns of natural resource distribution worldwide.

üåç Resource Distribution Patterns:

  • Uneven Distribution: Resources are not equally spread across Earth
  • Geological Factors: Rock type, age, tectonic activity affect resources
  • Climate Influence: Temperature and precipitation affect renewable resources
  • Economic Implications: Resource-rich vs. resource-poor countries
  • Trade Dependencies: Countries import what they lack

üìä Activity: Resource Wealth Analysis

Research and compare the natural resource wealth of five different countries. Create graphs showing their major resources and discuss how this affects their economies and international relationships.

üìù Quick Check:

Explain why natural resources are distributed unevenly around the world. Give specific examples of how this affects international trade.

Lesson 2: Resource Extraction and Processing

Objective: Students will examine methods of resource extraction and their environmental impacts.

‚õèÔ∏è Extraction Methods:

  • Mining: Surface mining, underground mining, mountaintop removal
  • Oil/Gas Drilling: Wells, offshore platforms, hydraulic fracturing
  • Forestry: Clear-cutting, selective harvesting, sustainable practices
  • Fishing: Commercial fishing, aquaculture, overfishing concerns
  • Environmental Costs: Habitat destruction, pollution, climate impacts

‚öñÔ∏è Activity: Extraction Impact Assessment

Choose one resource extraction method and create a detailed analysis of its environmental, economic, and social impacts. Include both benefits and costs to communities and ecosystems.

üìù Quick Check:

Compare two different methods of extracting the same resource (e.g., surface vs. underground mining). Discuss the trade-offs between environmental impact and economic efficiency.

Lesson 3: Energy Systems and Infrastructure

Objective: Students will analyze energy systems and the infrastructure needed to deliver energy.

üîå Energy Infrastructure:

  • Power Generation: Power plants, wind farms, solar installations
  • Transmission: High-voltage power lines, transformers
  • Distribution: Local power grids, substations
  • Storage: Batteries, pumped hydro, compressed air
  • Alaska Challenges: Remote communities, harsh weather, long distances

üèóÔ∏è Activity: Design a Microgrid

Design an energy system for a remote Alaskan village. Consider local resources, seasonal variations, storage needs, and backup systems. Present your design with diagrams and justifications.

üìù Quick Check:

Explain the challenges of providing reliable energy to remote Alaskan communities and propose three solutions to improve energy security.

Lesson 4: Resource Economics and Trade

Objective: Students will examine how natural resources affect economic development and international trade.

üí∞ Resource Economics:

  • Resource Curse: How resource wealth can sometimes hinder development
  • Price Volatility: Commodity prices fluctuate dramatically
  • Economic Diversification: Reducing dependence on single resources
  • Value-Added Processing: Processing raw materials domestically
  • Sustainable Development: Balancing extraction with long-term prosperity

üìà Activity: Alaska Resource Economy Timeline

Create a timeline showing how different natural resources have shaped Alaska's economy from statehood to present. Include boom and bust cycles, and analyze current economic diversification efforts.

üìù Assessment: Resource Management Policy

Write a policy proposal for sustainable resource management in Alaska. Address economic benefits, environmental protection, and community impacts. Support your recommendations with data and examples.

üåü 8th Grade: Sustainable Resource Systems

Lesson 1: Circular Economy and Resource Efficiency

Objective: Students will analyze circular economy models and resource efficiency strategies.

‚ôªÔ∏è Circular Economy Principles:

  • Design for Durability: Products last longer, need fewer resources
  • Waste as Input: One process's waste becomes another's input
  • Material Recovery: Extract maximum value before disposal
  • Renewable Energy: Power systems with renewable sources
  • Ecosystem Services: Natural systems provide economic value

üîÑ Activity: Circular Economy Design Challenge

Design a circular economy system for a specific industry (e.g., electronics, clothing, food). Map resource flows, identify waste streams that could become inputs, and calculate potential efficiency gains.

üìù Quick Check:

Compare linear "take-make-dispose" models with circular economy approaches. Analyze the benefits and challenges of implementing circular systems.

Lesson 2: Renewable Energy Technology and Policy

Objective: Students will evaluate renewable energy technologies and policies for sustainable development.

üåû Advanced Renewable Technologies:

  • Grid Integration: Smart grids, demand response, storage systems
  • Efficiency Improvements: Higher conversion rates, lower costs
  • Emerging Technologies: Wave power, biomass, hydrogen fuel cells
  • Policy Mechanisms: Feed-in tariffs, renewable portfolio standards
  • Alaska Applications: Remote power, seasonal storage, hybrid systems

üéØ Activity: Renewable Energy Policy Analysis

Research renewable energy policies in three different countries or states. Analyze their effectiveness, costs, and applicability to Alaska. Present recommendations for Alaska's renewable energy future.

üìù Quick Check:

Evaluate the potential for different renewable energy sources in Alaska, considering technical feasibility, economic viability, and policy support needed.

Lesson 3: Resource Conflicts and Cooperation

Objective: Students will analyze how natural resources can lead to conflicts or cooperation between groups.

‚öîÔ∏è Resource-Related Conflicts:

  • Water Disputes: Rivers, aquifers crossing political boundaries
  • Energy Security: Dependence on imports, pipeline disputes
  • Mining Rights: Indigenous rights, environmental protection
  • Arctic Resources: Climate change opening new extraction opportunities
  • Cooperation Models: International agreements, resource sharing

ü§ù Activity: Resource Diplomacy Simulation

Participate in a simulation negotiating Arctic resource development agreements. Represent different stakeholders (countries, indigenous groups, environmental organizations, corporations) and work toward mutually beneficial solutions.

üìù Quick Check:

Analyze a current resource conflict and propose mechanisms for peaceful resolution. Consider the interests of all stakeholders and potential win-win solutions.

Lesson 4: Future Resource Challenges and Innovations

Objective: Students will project future resource challenges and evaluate innovative solutions.

üöÄ Future Challenges and Innovations:

  • Population Growth: Increasing demand for resources
  • Climate Adaptation: Changing resource availability and needs
  • Technological Solutions: Artificial photosynthesis, carbon capture
  • Space Resources: Asteroid mining, lunar energy systems
  • Biotechnology: Engineered organisms for resource production

üîÆ Activity: Future Scenario Planning

Develop three scenarios for Alaska's resource future in 2060: business as usual, rapid renewable transition, and breakthrough technology. Analyze implications for economy, environment, and society.

üìù Assessment: Innovation Proposal

Design an innovative solution to a specific resource challenge facing Alaska. Include technical specifications, implementation timeline, cost-benefit analysis, and stakeholder engagement strategy. Present as a formal proposal to potential investors or policymakers.
