Unit Overview

This unit examines East Asia's rapid economic development, cultural traditions, and growing global influence, focusing on China, Japan, and Korea.

6th Grade Lessons üóæ

  1. Lesson 1: Geography of East Asia

    • Objective: Identify East Asian countries and major physical features.
    • Content: China, Japan, North Korea, South Korea, Mongolia, Taiwan.
    • Activities: East Asia mapping, physical features identification.
    • Assessment: Geography identification quiz.

  2. Lesson 2: Ancient Civilizations

    • Objective: Explore the rich history of East Asian civilizations.
    • Content: Chinese dynasties, Japanese samurai, Korean kingdoms.
    • Activities: Dynasty timeline, cultural achievements research.
    • Assessment: Ancient civilization presentation.

  3. Lesson 3: Cultural Traditions

    • Objective: Learn about East Asian cultural practices and beliefs.
    • Content: Confucianism, Buddhism, traditional arts, festivals.
    • Activities: Cultural comparison chart, festival celebration study.
    • Assessment: Cultural traditions project.

  4. Lesson 4: Modern Development

    • Objective: Compare modern economic development in East Asia.
    • Content: Economic growth, technology, urbanization, trade.
    • Activities: Development comparison, technology timeline.
    • Assessment: Modern East Asia poster.

7th Grade Lessons üè≠

  1. Lesson 1: Economic Transformation

    • Objective: Analyze East Asia's rapid economic growth and industrialization.
    • Content: Economic miracle, export-oriented growth, manufacturing hubs.
    • Activities: Economic growth analysis, industrial development study.
    • Assessment: Economic transformation report.

  2. Lesson 2: Environmental Challenges

    • Objective: Investigate environmental problems caused by rapid development.
    • Content: Air pollution, water contamination, deforestation, climate change.
    • Activities: Environmental impact study, solution brainstorming.
    • Assessment: Environmental action plan.

  3. Lesson 3: Population and Urbanization

    • Objective: Examine population policies and urban growth patterns.
    • Content: One-child policy, aging society, megacities, rural-urban migration.
    • Activities: Population data analysis, urbanization mapping.
    • Assessment: Population challenge evaluation.

  4. Lesson 4: Regional Cooperation and Tensions

    • Objective: Explore relationships between East Asian countries.
    • Content: Trade relationships, territorial disputes, cultural exchange.
    • Activities: Relationship mapping, conflict analysis.
    • Assessment: Regional dynamics report.

8th Grade Lessons üåè

  1. Lesson 1: Global Economic Power

    • Objective: Analyze East Asia's role in the global economy.
    • Content: Global supply chains, trade relationships, economic influence.
    • Activities: Global trade analysis, supply chain mapping.
    • Assessment: Economic power evaluation.

  2. Lesson 2: Technological Innovation

    • Objective: Investigate East Asia's technological advancement and innovation.
    • Content: Technology companies, R&D investment, digital society.
    • Activities: Innovation timeline, technology policy analysis.
    • Assessment: Technology strategy proposal.

  3. Lesson 3: Geopolitical Competition

    • Objective: Examine regional power competition and security issues.
    • Content: Military modernization, territorial disputes, alliance systems.
    • Activities: Security analysis, diplomatic strategy study.
    • Assessment: Geopolitical assessment report.

  4. Lesson 4: Cultural Soft Power

    • Objective: Evaluate East Asia's growing cultural influence globally.
    • Content: Pop culture, language learning, cultural diplomacy.
    • Activities: Cultural influence mapping, soft power analysis.
    • Assessment: Cultural influence evaluation.

Unit Assessment

  • 6th Grade: Geography quiz, civilization presentation, cultural project, modern poster.
  • 7th Grade: Transformation report, environmental plan, population evaluation, dynamics report.
  • 8th Grade: Economic power evaluation, technology proposal, geopolitical assessment, cultural influence analysis.
