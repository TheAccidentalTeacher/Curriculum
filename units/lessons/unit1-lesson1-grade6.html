6th Grade - Day 1

Estimated Time: 45 minutes

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will discover what geography is and why it matters in our daily lives.

📋 Materials Needed:

  • Paper and colored pencils/markers
  • Local maps (printed or digital)
  • Magazines for cutting (optional)

📖 Lesson Content

🌎 What is Geography?

Geography is the study of Earth and everything on it - the land, water, plants, animals, and people!

🏔️ Alaska Examples:

  • Physical Geography: Mount McKinley (Denali), glaciers, tundra
  • Human Geography: Anchorage city, fishing villages, oil pipelines
  • Fun Fact: Alaska has more coastline than all other U.S. states combined!

🎨 Activities

Activity 1: Draw Your Neighborhood Map (20 minutes)

Instructions:

  1. Draw your house in the center of your paper
  2. Add important places around your house (school, store, friends' houses)
  3. Include natural features (trees, hills, rivers)
  4. Add streets and pathways
  5. Use colors to make it interesting!
💡 Teacher Tip: Walk around and help students think about what they see every day on their way to school.

Activity 2: "Geography in My Life" Discussion (15 minutes)

Discussion Questions:

  • How do you use geography every day?
  • What would happen if we didn't have maps?
  • How does Alaska's geography affect how we live?

Share Examples: Getting to school, weather affecting our clothes, where food comes from
