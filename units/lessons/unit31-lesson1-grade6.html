üé® Visual Learning: Geography Sorting

Scaffolding: Start with obvious examples, gradually add complex cases

  • Use picture cards showing various geographic features
  • Students sort into "Physical" and "Human" categories
  • Discuss borderline cases as a class
  • Create a classroom anchor chart with examples

Differentiation: Provide base maps for struggling learners

  • Draw a map of the school and surrounding area

  • Include a legend and compass rose
  • Share maps with partner for peer feedback

üìù Assessment Strategies

Formative Assessment Strategies:

  • Exit Ticket: Name one physical and one human feature you saw today
  • Thumbs Up/Down: Quick check for understanding during lesson
  • Peer Sharing: Students explain concepts to a partner
  • Visual Assessment: Drawing or labeling activities

Summative Assessment:

Geography Identification Quiz (Scaffolded):

  1. Look at the picture. Is this physical or human geography? Explain why.
  2. Give one example of physical geography from Alaska.
  3. Give one example of human geography from your community.
